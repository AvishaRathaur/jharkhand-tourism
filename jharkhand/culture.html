Vibrant Culture

Folk Dances

Jharkhand's culture is alive with rhythmic folk dances. The Chhau dance is a powerful, semi-classical form with martial arts elements, famous for its elaborate masks and costumes that depict mythological stories. Other popular dances include the Santhali dance, performed with vibrant traditional attire, and the Karma dance, which is performed around a sacred tree during the Karma festival to pray for good fortune and harvest.

Handicrafts & Art

The state is a hub of indigenous art and craft. The tribal communities are skilled in creating beautiful items from bamboo, wood, and metal. Dokra art, a non-ferrous metal casting technique, is used to create intricate figurines and jewelry. Sohrai and Khovar paintings are traditional mural art forms practiced by women, adorning the walls of their homes with natural pigments and motifs inspired by nature.

Local Cuisine

Jharkhandi cuisine is simple, earthy, and delicious. Staple dishes include Dhuska (deep-fried rice and lentil pancakes), Litti Chokha, and Rugra (an indigenous mushroom). Thekua is a popular sweet snack made from wheat flour and jaggery. The use of locally sourced ingredients and traditional cooking methods gives the food a unique and authentic flavor.
