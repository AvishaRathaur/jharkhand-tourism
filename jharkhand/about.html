About Jharkhand

History & Heritage

Jharkhand, known as the 'Land of Forests', was formed on 15 November 2000. The region has a rich and ancient history, with archaeological finds dating back to the Mesolithic-Chalcolithic period. It has been a part of several powerful empires, including the Magadha and Maurya empires. The region is famed for its role in India's freedom struggle, with tribal leaders like Birsa Munda leading significant uprisings against British colonial rule. This legacy of resilience and cultural pride is a cornerstone of Jharkhand's identity.

Geography & Nature

Situated on the Chota Nagpur Plateau, Jharkhand is blessed with an undulating landscape of hills, plateaus, and stunning valleys. The state is covered in dense tropical forests, which are home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and sloth bears, many of which are protected in sanctuaries like Betla National Park. Jharkhand is also famous for its spectacular waterfalls, such as Hundru, Dassam, and Jonha Falls, which cascade down the plateau's edges, creating breathtaking sights, especially during the monsoon season.
